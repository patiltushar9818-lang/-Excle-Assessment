1. What is the first step an analyst should take before opening a dataset to investigate flat revenue despite increased traffic?
	1. The Pre-Analysis:- 
	First Step:-
		An analyst should formulate a clear hypothesis before opening the data.
		For a scenario with increased traffic but flat revenue, 
		the analyst should brainstorm likely failure points in the conversion funnel. 
		This prevents directionless "data fishing" and structures the investigation.
		Conversion Drop: Is higher traffic bringing low-quality visitors who do not buy?Pricing/Discounting: Did average order values drop due to heavy promotional discounts?
		Technical Issues: Is a broken checkout page causing high cart abandonment?


2. What specific characteristics distinguish a "Key Performance Indicator" (KPI) from a standard business metric?
	Feature    	Standard Business Metric         			Key Performance Indicator (KPI)

	Focus	    	Tracks general business activity.			Measures progress toward core strategic goals.
	Actionability 	Informational; does not immediately trigger changes. 	Triggers immediate action if targets are missed.
	Context		Raw volume or standalone counts.			Often a ratio or rate with a defined target/benchmark.
	Example		Total Page Views.					Conversion Rate.


3. Classify Average Order Value (AOV) and Customer Repeat Rate as either Descriptive or Diagnostic KPIs and state the questions they answer.

	Average Order Value (AOV): 
		Diagnostic KPIQuestion Answered: "How much are customers spending per transaction?" 
		It diagnoses why revenue might be flat (e.g., traffic is up, but people are buying cheaper items).

	Customer Repeat Rate: 
		Diagnostic KPIQuestion Answered: "Are we retaining existing customers or relying entirely on new traffic?" 
		It diagnoses the health of customer loyalty and long-term revenue sustainability.


4. Provide one justified reason to choose Excel and one reason to choose PowerBI for analyzing Monthly Revenue.
	Why choose Excel: 
		It is ideal for ad-hoc, flexible modeling and rapid prototyping. 
		If an executive needs a quick, one-off financial forecast or manual adjustments to monthly numbers, 
		Excel’s cell-level editing and pivot tables are faster to deploy without setting up a formal data infrastructure.

	Why choose Power BI: 
		It is ideal for automated, scalable dashboarding and multi-source integration. 
		Power BI handles millions of rows effortlessly, schedules automatic data refreshes, 
		secures data with role-based access, and allows interactive filtering across complex, related data tables.


5. Is "Number of Customers" a valid Diagnostic KPI for revenue trends? Justify by comparing Descriptive vs. Diagnostic logic.
	No, "Number of Customers" is not a valid Diagnostic KPI; it is a Descriptive metric.

	Descriptive logic:- 
		tells you what happened. "Number of Customers" simply counts the volume of unique purchasers in a given timeframe. 
		It states a result.

	Diagnostic logic:-
		tells you why it happened. 
		To make it diagnostic, 
		it must be transformed into a rate or ratio—such as Customer Acquisition Rate or Churn Rate—which explains the underlying momentum causing the revenue trend to shift.


6. Why is treating raw data columns (like UnitPrice) as KPIs incorrect, and what transformation steps are required to create a meaningful KPI?
	Treating a raw column like UnitPrice as a KPI is incorrect because raw data lacks aggregation, time context, and business performance benchmarks. 
	A single row's unit price does not tell you if the business is succeeding overall.
	To transform UnitPrice into a meaningful KPI, an analyst must follow these three steps:
	Aggregate: 
		Combine the raw column with other data to create a metric (e.g., UnitPrice \(\times \) Quantity = Total Revenue).
	Contextualise: 
		Filter or group the aggregation over a defined period (e.g., Monthly Total Revenue).
	Benchmark: 
		Compare the final calculation against a target, budget, or prior period (e.g., Monthly Revenue vs. Target %). 
		This final step creates the true KPI.
